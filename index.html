Wall Type Notes:
CHB Wall: Mortar mix ratio typically 1:4 (cement:sand). Reinforcement spacing depends on structural design (e.g., every 3rd layer). Plastering is optional for aesthetic finish.
Brick Wall: Mortar mix ratio typically 1:6 (cement:sand). Bricks vary in size; adjust quantities accordingly. Reinforcement used for structural walls.
Glass Block Wall: Use specialized glass block mortar for better adhesion. Spacers ensure uniform gaps; sealant for weatherproofing. Reinforcement for stability, especially in larger walls.
AAC Block Wall: Thin-bed mortar reduces material use compared to CHB. AAC blocks are lightweight; check for load-bearing requirements. Specialized plaster may be needed for smooth finish.
SIP Wall: SIPS are pre-insulated; minimal additional insulation needed. Framing lumber for panel connections. Sealant ensures airtight joints.
Precast Concrete Wall: Panels are factory-made; size varies by design. Grout and sealant for joint sealing and stability. Minimal on-site materials due to prefabrication.
